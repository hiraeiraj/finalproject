The Strokes

Formed 1998 in New York City, New york

The Strokes are an American rock band formed in New York City in 1998. The group was formed by lead vocalist Julian Casablancas, guitarist Nick Valensi, guitarist Albert Hammond Jr., bassist Nikolai Fraiture, and drummer Fabrizio Moretti. Their formation marked the beginning of a new wave of garage rock revival, with their sound characterized by raw guitar riffs, catchy melodies, and a minimalist approach to production.

The band gained widespread attention with the release of their debut album Is This It in 2001, which is widely regarded as one of the most influential albums of the early 2000s. The Strokes' distinctive blend of retro rock influences, such as the Velvet Underground and Television, with a modern twist helped them capture the attention of both critics and fans.

Over the years, they've released several critically acclaimed albums, including Room on Fire (2003) and First Impressions of Earth (2006), while evolving their sound and maintaining a strong presence in the music industry. The Strokes have remained a key figure in shaping the modern rock scene, continuing to influence new generations of musicians.

Julian Casablancas, Lead Singer

Julian Casablancas is the lead singer and main songwriter of The Strokes. Born in 1978 in New York City, he formed the band in 1998 with high school friends. His gritty, drawling vocals and introspective lyrics became central to their breakthrough debut Is This It (2001), which helped ignite the early 2000s garage rock revival. Julian’s solo career includes the synth-heavy Phrazes for the Young, and he later formed The Voidz to explore more experimental sounds. Despite his side projects, he remains the creative engine of The Strokes, known for his unique voice, poetic style, and rebellious charisma.

Albert Hammond Jr, Guitarist

Albert Hammond Jr. is The Strokes’ rhythm and lead guitarist, known for his sharp, melodic riffs. Born in 1980 in Los Angeles to musician Albert Hammond Sr., he moved to New York for school and reconnected with Julian Casablancas. His guitar work helps define the band's tight, catchy sound, particularly on early tracks like “Last Nite.” Albert launched a solo career in 2006, releasing several critically praised albums blending rock and pop influences. He's also known for his sharp fashion sense and signature red Fender Stratocaster. Albert remains a vital creative force in the band and beyond.

Nick Valensi, Guitarist

Nick Valensi is the lead guitarist of The Strokes, celebrated for his stylish solos and use of effects. Born in 1981 in New York City, he helped shape the band’s dual-guitar sound with Albert Hammond Jr., creating sharp, layered textures on songs like “Reptilia” and “Juicebox.” Nick plays a custom Epiphone Riviera, which has become iconic in indie circles. In 2016, he formed CRX, a side project with a heavier sound. Though more private than other members, Nick’s musicianship is a cornerstone of The Strokes’ identity, blending rock precision with a cool, effortless tone that fans instantly recognize.

Nikoli Fraiture, Bassist

Nikolai Fraiture is the bassist for The Strokes, providing steady, understated grooves that support the band’s rhythm-driven sound. Born in 1978 in New York City, he joined the group during their early days and quickly became an essential piece of their tight, minimalist aesthetic. His basslines, especially on tracks like “Someday” and “Hard to Explain,” complement the guitars perfectly without overpowering the mix. Outside the band, Nikolai has explored solo music under the name Nickel Eye. Soft-spoken and artistic, he’s also involved in visual art and poetry, contributing a thoughtful presence to The Strokes’ dynamic.

Fabrizio Moretti, Drummer

Fabrizio Moretti is the drummer for The Strokes, known for his punchy, precise playing and danceable rhythms. Born in 1980 in Brazil and raised in New York, he met the band while attending school with Julian Casablancas. His minimalist drumming style helped shape the band’s raw, energetic sound, especially on hits like “Last Nite” and “Is This It.” Fab brings a loose, instinctive feel to the group, balancing tight grooves with spontaneity. Outside The Strokes, he’s played in other bands like Little Joy and Machinegum. Charismatic and creative, Fab is as much a fan favorite as he is a rhythmic backbone.
