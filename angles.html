Angles

Released on March 18, 2011

Angles, released in 2011, was The Strokes’ “we’re back… sort of” album. After a bit of a break — and some behind-the-scenes drama — the band returned with a record that sounded like five guys trying to figure each other out again. It’s experimental, kind of scattered, but still full of those moments where everything clicks. This time, the process was messy. Julian wasn’t always in the studio with the rest of the band — he recorded a lot of his vocals separately, which caused some tension. But weirdly, that distance also gave the album this cool, fractured vibe. Angles jumps around stylistically, pulling from new wave, synth-pop, and even reggae-ish grooves (“Machu Picchu,” anyone?). Songs like “Under Cover of Darkness” reminded fans why they fell in love with The Strokes in the first place — catchy, punchy, and effortlessly cool. But then you’ve got tracks like “Games” or “Call Me Back” that sound like they’re from another planet entirely (in a good way). Angles might not be the band’s tightest album, but it’s one of their most interesting. It’s what happens when a group tries to grow, experiment, and reconnect — even if it’s a little awkward along the way. And honestly? That makes it kind of special.
